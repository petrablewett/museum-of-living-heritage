Livingstone Museum

Zambia's oldest and largest museum

A National Treasure

Livingstone Museum is Zambia's oldest and largest museum, established in 1934 in the historic town of Livingstone near Victoria Falls. Named after the famous explorer David Livingstone, the museum houses an extraordinary collection spanning archaeology, ethnography, history, and natural history.

The museum's collections include artifacts from David Livingstone's expeditions, including personal items and journals that document his famous journeys through central Africa. Visitors can explore exhibits on Zambia's prehistoric past, traditional cultures, colonial history, and the journey to independence.

The ethnography galleries showcase the diverse cultural heritage of Zambia's many ethnic groups, with displays of traditional crafts, clothing, musical instruments, and ceremonial objects. The natural history section features specimens of Zambia's wildlife and geological exhibits including fossils and minerals.

Visiting the Museum

Livingstone Museum is located on Mosi-oa-Tunya Road in Livingstone, Southern Province. The museum is open daily and offers a comprehensive introduction to Zambia's heritage. Combined with a visit to nearby Victoria Falls, the museum makes for an essential cultural experience in this historic town.
