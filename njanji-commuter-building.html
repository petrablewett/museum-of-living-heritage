Njanji Commuter Building

A gathering place for independence

Heart of the Independence Movement

The Njanji Commuter Building holds a special place in Zambia's history as an important gathering place during the struggle for independence. Located in Lusaka, this historic structure served as a meeting point where workers and activists came together to organize and discuss the future of their nation.

During the late colonial period, the building witnessed key moments in the independence movement. It was here that ordinary Zambians - commuters, workers, and citizens - gathered to hear speeches, share news, and build the solidarity that would eventually lead to the birth of an independent Zambia on October 24, 1964.

The building represents the grassroots nature of Zambia's liberation struggle, reminding us that independence was won not just by political leaders but by the collective efforts of everyday people who dreamed of freedom and self-determination.

Visiting the Site

The Njanji Commuter Building is located in Lusaka and can be visited as part of a tour of the city's independence-era heritage sites. The building stands as a testament to the power of collective action and the importance of preserving the memory of those who fought for Zambia's freedom.
