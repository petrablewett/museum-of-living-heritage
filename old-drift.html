Old Drift Cemetery

A haunting memorial to Zambia's colonial pioneers

Historical Significance

The Old Drift Cemetery stands as a poignant reminder of the challenges faced by early European settlers near Victoria Falls. Located on the banks of the Zambezi River, this colonial-era graveyard is the final resting place of pioneers, traders, and adventurers who came to this region in the late 19th and early 20th centuries.

Old Drift was the original settlement near Victoria Falls before Livingstone town was established on higher ground. The low-lying location proved deadly - malaria and blackwater fever claimed many lives. The weathered headstones tell stories of young lives cut short, families torn apart, and the harsh realities of colonial expansion.

Today, the cemetery is maintained as a national monument, offering visitors a contemplative space to reflect on the human cost of exploration and settlement in this remote corner of Africa.

Visiting the Cemetery

Old Drift Cemetery is located within the Mosi-oa-Tunya National Park, a short drive from Livingstone. The site is accessible year-round and offers a peaceful, shaded setting among indigenous trees. Guided tours are available and provide deeper insights into the stories of those buried here.
