Chirundu Fossil Forest

Petrified remnants of ancient woodlands

A Window into Deep Time

The Chirundu Fossil Forest is a remarkable geological site in Southern Province, featuring petrified tree trunks that date back millions of years. These fossilized remains offer a fascinating glimpse into the ancient landscapes and climate conditions that once existed in the Zambezi Valley long before humans walked the earth.

The petrified wood at Chirundu was formed when ancient trees were buried by sediment and, over millions of years, their organic material was gradually replaced by minerals - primarily silica - preserving the original structure of the wood in stunning detail. Visitors can see the preserved grain patterns, growth rings, and even bark textures in these stone trees.

The fossil forest provides important evidence about prehistoric ecosystems in southern Africa, revealing that this region once supported lush forests very different from today's landscape. The site helps scientists understand climate change over geological time scales and the evolution of plant life on the continent.

Visiting the Site

The Chirundu Fossil Forest is located in Southern Province near the town of Chirundu, close to the Zambezi River and the border with Zimbabwe. The fossilized trees are scattered across an open area and can be explored on foot. The best time to visit is during the dry season when access roads are more reliable. Visitors should bring water and sun protection as the site offers limited shade.
