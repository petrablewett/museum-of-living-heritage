Nachikufu Cave

15,000 years of human history

A Window into Prehistory

Nachikufu Cave is one of Zambia's most important archaeological sites, containing evidence of human occupation spanning over 15,000 years. Located in Muchinga Province, this remarkable rock shelter has yielded crucial insights into the lives of our Stone Age ancestors.

The cave gave its name to the "Nachikufan" stone tool industry, a distinctive Later Stone Age culture identified by archaeologists. The site contains rich deposits of stone tools, pottery fragments, and other artifacts that tell the story of how ancient peoples lived, hunted, and adapted to their environment.

The walls of the cave feature ancient rock paintings, adding an artistic dimension to the archaeological record. These paintings provide glimpses into the spiritual beliefs and daily lives of the cave's prehistoric inhabitants.

Visiting the Cave

Nachikufu Cave is located near Mpika in Muchinga Province. The site is protected as a National Monument, and visitors can explore the cave with permission from local authorities. The rock paintings and archaeological significance make it a must-visit for anyone interested in Zambia's deep history.
