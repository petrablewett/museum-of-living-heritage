Leopards Hill Cave

Stone Age secrets near Lusaka

A Window into the Stone Age

Leopards Hill Cave is an important archaeological site located in the hills southeast of Lusaka, providing valuable evidence of Stone Age occupation in central Zambia. The cave and its surrounding rock shelters have yielded rich deposits of artifacts that help tell the story of human prehistory in this region.

Archaeological excavations at Leopards Hill have uncovered stone tools, pottery fragments, and other artifacts spanning thousands of years of human habitation. The site has contributed significantly to our understanding of how ancient peoples lived, hunted, and adapted to their environment in the Lusaka area.

The cave's location in the distinctive kopje landscape of the Lusaka region made it an ideal shelter for prehistoric communities. The surrounding hills would have provided vantage points for spotting game, while the cave offered protection from the elements and predators.

Visiting the Site

Leopards Hill Cave is located approximately 25 kilometers southeast of Lusaka city center. The site can be visited with permission and offers a fascinating glimpse into Zambia's prehistoric past just a short distance from the capital. The surrounding area features scenic hill country and diverse wildlife.
